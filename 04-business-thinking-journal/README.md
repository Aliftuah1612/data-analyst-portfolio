# 🧠 The Business Thinking Journal

This is the section that shows **how I learn like an analyst, not just use the tools.**

Each entry is a scenario/simulation: I pick **one real company**, then work through three questions:

1. **Why do these KPIs matter?** — Which metrics would this company's leadership actually watch, and why those?
2. **What insights would I surface?** — Given the business model, what patterns in the data would be worth escalating?
3. **What business problem gets solved?** — How does the analysis connect to a decision: pricing, retention, expansion, cost?

## Entry format

```
YYYY-MM-company-name/
└── README.md
    ├── 1. Company & business model (in one paragraph)
    ├── 2. The KPIs — and why they matter for THIS business
    ├── 3. Hypothetical insights I'd hunt for
    ├── 4. The business problem this solves
    └── 5. What I'd need: data, caveats, next questions
```

## The point

Tools change. SQL dialects change. What doesn't change is the ability to look at a business and ask: *what decision needs to be made, and what would the data need to say to make it confidently?*
